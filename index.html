Executive Summary

Original Problem

Original Problem

Many people consume energy daily in an unplanned and random way, often resulting in unexpectedly high bills and sometimes power disconnections due to late payments. Additionally, users do not fully benefit from renewable energy sources and energy-saving solutions, either because of lack of awareness or the complexity of current systems. These challenges are even greater for vulnerable groups such as the elderly, people with disabilities, and those with low technological literacy, who face barriers when using existing complex solutions. At a broader level, countries and energy providers often produce and distribute energy based on estimates rather than real data analysis, leading to overproduction, shortages, and inefficiencies in national planning, imports, and exports.

Proposed Solution

We developed a next-generation smart energy ecosystem that combines Artificial Intelligence (AI), the Internet of Things (IoT), psychology, and behavioral science to create a human-centered solution. Our system does not only monitor energy use — it learns, predicts, and adapts to users' daily habits and emotional states, while also helping governments make data-driven decisions about production and export.

Key features
  • AI models such as RNN for forecasting, Isolation Forest for anomaly detection, and XGBoost for behavior optimization.
  • Designed with accessibility in mind, featuring a multilingual and voice-controlled interface suitable for elderly users and those with disabilities.
  • Smart reminders to assist users in paying bills on time, helping prevent service interruptions.
  • Actionable suggestions for adopting renewable energy and energy-saving devices to reduce overall costs.
  • Dashboards tailored for government use, supporting energy production planning based on real consumption data.
  • Privacy-first approach with edge AI and offline functionality, catering to areas with limited internet connectivity.
  • Manual data input such as meter readings and bills, enhancing prediction accuracy and personalization.

        • Problem Overview

        Energy and sustainability: covers nationwide energy production forecasting, consumption monitoring in offices, factories, and homes — with a practical IoT application focused on smart homes to help individual users manage consumption and avoid power disconnections.

        Unplanned and inefficient energy consumption in homes, offices, and factories leads to high, unpredictable costs and sometimes power cuts. At the national level, energy production is often based on estimates rather than real data, resulting in either overproduction (waste) or shortages. While advanced systems exist, they are often too complex and not designed inclusively — especially excluding elderly users, people with disabilities, and those unfamiliar with technology. Our IoT part specifically targets homes to help users monitor consumption in real time, receive smart reminders, and get personalized suggestions to avoid disconnections and reduce costs.

        • Affects millions of households, businesses, and national infrastructure.
        • Financial impact: unexpected high bills, production inefficiency, and economic waste.
        • Service impact: power cuts that can affect homes, critical offices, and factories.
        • Social impact: digital exclusion of elderly, disabled, and low-tech users.
        • Environmental impact: overproduction increases carbon emissions; underproduction causes shortages.

        Reference & Role of Data Forecasting

        We explored the article "How Ard's Solar Forecasting Model Outshone the Competition" which shows how AI models can accurately forecast solar energy production using real weather data and consumption patterns.

        This approach helps balance supply and demand, prevents sudden shortages, and improves national energy planning.

        In our project, similar AI forecasting models help:

        • Predict daily energy production (e.g., kWh from solar panels).
        • Predict user consumption.
        • Provide alerts and suggestions to users.
        • Help governments plan production, export, and import based on real predictions.

        By integrating forecasting, we move from reactive to proactive energy management — reducing waste, improving affordability, and supporting sustainability.
